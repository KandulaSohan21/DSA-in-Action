# Real-Life Use Cases of Queue

Queues are used whenever requests or tasks should be processed in the same order they arrive.

---

## 1. Hospital Token Management

Patients receive token numbers upon arrival.

The patient who receives the first token is called first for consultation.

---

## 2. Ticket Booking Counters

People standing in line purchase tickets in the order they arrived.

---

## 3. Printer Queue

Multiple users send print jobs to a printer.

The first document sent is printed first.

---

## 4. Customer Support Systems

Support tickets are handled in the order they are received.

---

## 5. Food Delivery Orders

Restaurants prepare orders in the order customers place them.

---

## 6. CPU Process Scheduling

Operating systems schedule processes waiting for execution using queue-based scheduling techniques.

---

## 7. Breadth-First Search (BFS)

BFS uses a queue to visit graph nodes level by level.

---

## Why Learn Queue?

Queues form the backbone of many real-world systems where fairness and sequential processing are required, such as operating systems, networking, banking, healthcare, and web servers.
