# Real-Life Use Cases of Linear Search

Linear Search is commonly used when datasets are small or unsorted.

## 1. Contact Search in Mobile Phone

When searching for a contact in an unsorted contact list, the system may check names one by one until it finds the desired contact.

Example:
Searching for "Rahul" among saved contacts.

---

## 2. Attendance Verification

Teachers often verify whether a student's roll number exists in an attendance list.

Example:
Checking whether Roll No. 27 is present.

---

## 3. Product Lookup in Small Stores

Small inventory systems search products sequentially when the product database is small.

Example:
Finding "Milk" in a grocery inventory.

---

## 4. Employee Record Search

Organizations may search employee IDs from a small employee database.

Example:
Searching Employee ID EMP102.

---

## 5. Playlist Song Search

Music players can search for a song title within a playlist.

Example:
Finding "Shape of You" in a playlist.

---

## 6. Library Book Availability

A librarian checks whether a specific book exists in the available books list.

Example:
Searching for "Data Structures and Algorithms".

---

## 7. Examination Result Lookup

Searching for a student's registration number in a result sheet.

Example:
Finding Registration Number 230401.

---

## Why Linear Search Is Still Used

Even though advanced algorithms exist, Linear Search remains useful because:

* Data is often unsorted.
* Datasets may be small.
* Implementation is simple.
* No preprocessing is required.
