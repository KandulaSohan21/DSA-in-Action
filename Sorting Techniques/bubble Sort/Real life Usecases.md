# Real-Life Use Cases of Bubble Sort

Although Bubble Sort is rarely used in large production systems, it helps demonstrate how sorting works in many real-world scenarios.

---

## 1. Ranking Songs by Popularity

A music platform may sort songs based on play counts.

Example:

Before Sorting:

* Song A → 450 Plays
* Song B → 900 Plays
* Song C → 300 Plays

After Sorting:

* Song B → 900 Plays
* Song A → 450 Plays
* Song C → 300 Plays

---

## 2. Student Leaderboard

Schools can sort students based on marks.

Example:

Sorting students from highest to lowest score.

---

## 3. Product Price Arrangement

An online store can sort products by price.

Example:

₹500, ₹200, ₹1000, ₹300

Sorted:

₹200, ₹300, ₹500, ₹1000

---

## 4. Sports Rankings

Teams can be ranked based on points scored.

Example:

Sorting teams from highest to lowest points.

---

## 5. Movie Ratings

Streaming platforms can display movies sorted by ratings.

Example:

8.9, 7.5, 9.2, 8.1

Sorted:

9.2, 8.9, 8.1, 7.5

---

## Why Learn Bubble Sort?

Bubble Sort is usually not the fastest sorting algorithm, but it introduces fundamental sorting concepts such as:

* Comparison
* Swapping
* Iteration
* Optimization

These concepts are used in many advanced sorting algorithms.
